New York City has long served as a major publishing center in the history of Latin American and Latinx literature. At the end of the nineteenth century, the city’s Latin American publishing community was centered in [the very neighborhood that surrounds Pace University.](https://g.page/pace-university-new-york?share) Understanding lower Manhattan’s role as a publishing center for Latin Americans living in New York at that time will provide us with examples of the ways in which print history can inform our understanding of the formation of Latinx literature and culture.
